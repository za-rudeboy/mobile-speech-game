# Practical Activity Backlog

## Purpose
This backlog translates speech and language support goals for Caelum into short, app-friendly activity ideas. It is not a full therapy plan. It is a practical product backlog for high-value interactions that fit a parent-guided app.

## Child Profile Anchors
- age 6
- uses some full sentences, but not consistently
- often uses shorter 2 to 3 word utterances
- pronunciation is reduced, so intelligibility can break down
- struggles with some relational and abstract concepts like `next to`
- understands familiar directions better than novel ones
- usually benefits from visual demonstration
- concentration varies and movement breaks help

## Shared Design Rules
- keep each task between 30 and 90 seconds
- show the visual before or with the spoken prompt
- keep spoken instructions short and concrete
- support touch on every task
- offer a model, then guided practice, then independent try
- include visible `help`, `break`, and `show me again` supports
- reward communication attempts, not only perfect speech

## Priority 1: Daily Phrase Practice
### Goal
Improve clarity of high-frequency functional phrases that matter in daily routines.

### Interaction Loop
1. Show a familiar scene.
2. Play a short model phrase.
3. Let the child repeat it or tap to hear it again.
4. Use the same phrase in a simple choice or action.

### Example Content
- `i want a banana please`
- `help me please`
- `my hand hurts`
- `all done`

### Why It Matters
This supports intelligibility without forcing isolated sound drills that may not transfer to daily life.

## Priority 2: Repair And Regulation Buttons
### Goal
Teach Caelum to ask for support when he does not understand or is not understood.

### Interaction Loop
1. The app gives a prompt.
2. If the child hesitates or gets it wrong, show support options immediately.
3. The child taps `help`, `break`, or `show me again`.
4. The app responds with a short scaffold or a short movement pause.

### Example Content
- `help`
- `break`
- `show me again`
- `say again`

### Why It Matters
This reduces frustration and gives him a communication strategy that works inside and outside the app.

## Priority 3: Where Is It?
### Goal
Build reliable understanding and use of core relational concepts.

### Interaction Loop
1. Show a simple scene with 2 to 3 objects.
2. Give one spoken prompt such as `Put the cat under the table`.
3. The child drags or taps the correct placement.
4. The app models the answer using the same concept.

### Example Content
- `in`
- `on`
- `under`
- `next to`

### Why It Matters
These concepts are practical, visual, and high value for both comprehension and expression.

## Priority 4: Do What I Say
### Goal
Improve success with novel 1-step directions, then short 2-step directions in familiar routines.

### Interaction Loop
1. Show a scene with clear action targets.
2. Give one short instruction.
3. If needed, offer a visual demonstration on the first trial.
4. Fade support on later trials.

### Example Content
- `Put the apple in the box`
- `Give the ball to the boy`
- `Take the hat and put it on the bed`

### Why It Matters
This directly targets a current weakness and fits the need for visual support.

## Priority 5: Build The Sentence
### Goal
Expand short utterances into more reliable functional sentence frames.

### Interaction Loop
1. Show picture tiles and a visible sentence starter.
2. The child chooses the missing word or picture.
3. The app reads the whole sentence.
4. The child says the sentence or repeats a short chunk.

### Example Content
- `I want ___`
- `I need help`
- `It is next to the ___`
- `My ___ hurts`

### Why It Matters
This bridges single words and short phrases into practical sentences without demanding open-ended speech.

## Priority 6: Picture Questions
### Goal
Answer simple `what`, `where`, and `who` questions from one picture or short action scene.

### Interaction Loop
1. Show one clear picture.
2. Ask one question.
3. Start with two choices if needed.
4. Move toward spoken or open responses later.

### Example Content
- `What is he eating?`
- `Where is the car?`
- `Who has the ball?`

### Why It Matters
This supports receptive language, question understanding, and early expressive flexibility.

## Priority 7: Movement Search
### Goal
Use movement to support attention, comprehension, and concept learning.

### Interaction Loop
1. Give a short task that requires a physical response.
2. Let the child find, tap, point, or stand to act it out.
3. End with a short confirming phrase.

### Example Content
- `Find the one under the chair`
- `Tap the big one`
- `Stand up and give it to me`

### Why It Matters
This matches his concentration profile and makes practice less sedentary.

## Suggested Build Order
1. `Where Is It?`
2. `Daily Phrase Practice`
3. `Repair And Regulation Buttons`
4. `Do What I Say`
5. `Build The Sentence`
6. `Picture Questions`
7. `Movement Search`

## Product Notes
- Do not choose exact speech sound targets from the app alone. Those should come from a real speech sample and stimulability check with an SLP.
- The app can still support intelligibility by modeling functional phrases, chunking them, and reinforcing slower, clearer attempts.
- If a task becomes frustrating, the app should shorten the demand rather than repeating the same failed prompt.
